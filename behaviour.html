Believe it or not

In Russia you can get in trouble for smiling. “Russia wants to melt its reputation as the world's iciest superpower- by training workers on how to smile at tourists who are visiting for the World Cup, according to reports.”, says a confirmed site- the new york post.

 

Russians do not smile as much as other Europeans, according to the BBC. An article in The Atlantic attempts to explain why- because it translates a Russian proverb: "Laughing for no reason is a sign of stupidity."

Temperature and emotions

The most well known feature of the Russian climate is that it has extremely cold weather all-year round.

With high latitudes, arctic winds, and frozen coastlines makes it the 6th coldest place to live in.

"Cold temperatures can influence our thoughts and decisions without even knowing it," says Laura Geggel in a 'Live Science article.

Getting straight to the point is considered a vital survival skill when you want to keep out of the cold, simple as that.

Words are silver, but silence is golden

“Words are silver, but silence is golden” - this proverb is still very popular among Russians.

In Russia, certain words became a criminal offense as early as in the 16th century. So it's better to only speak only when addressed.

Words are very, very important in Russian culture, so don't waste them if you want to pass off as a local.

Deep Inside

Deep Inside:

On the surface they might not appear as social butterflies, but deep inside they are.

When it's safe, when they're around friends and family they are loud, spontaneous and extremely generous.

This has been part of their tradition for a long time- Children usually open their gifts in private but adults do it in front of you- this seems to be a sign of respect.

“Friendship”, they value this quality more than anything else. “Blood is thicker than water”, this proverb is not relevant in Russian culture.
